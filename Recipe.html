Lean Mean Turkey Burgers & Mom's Mac'n'Cheese

A little bit of healthy, a little bit of soul food and a whole lot of flavor. Burgers with a side of Mom's Mac is one of my favorite meals. Just as easy to make for a weekend party or a weeknight meal after a long day of work, everyone will be satisfied and asking for more!

How To Make It

Shopping List

Burgers

Mac
  • 1lb. - Ground Turkey Meat
  • 1 Package - Whole Wheat Buns
  • Garlic Powder
  • 1tbs. - Gourmet Burger Seasoning by Weber Grills
  • Steak Seasoning of Your Choice
  • 1 Head/Package - Lettuce or Spinach
  • 1 - Tomato
  • Ketchup & Mustard
  • Salt & Pepper
  • 1/2lb - Elbow Macaroni
  • 1 1/2lbs - Cheddar Cheese
  • 1/2 Stick - Butter
  • 3 - Large Eggs
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Deep Baking Dish

Prep Work

Burgers

  1. Place meat in large mixing bowl
  2. Add burger seasoning
  3. Add steak seasoning and garlic powder to taste
  4. Add one squirt of ketchup (for moisture)
  5. Mix ingredients in bowl thoroughly
  6. Once mixed, form patties from the mixture by rolling half a handful of meat into a ball and pressing into palm. This should make about 4 or 5 patties depending on size
  7. Pre-heat charcoal or gas grill to medium-high heat

Mac

  1. Boil macaroni according to instructions
  2. Preheat Oven to 350o
  3. Shred all of the cheese into a separate bowl
  4. Crack eggs into separate bowl and wisk together thoroughly
  5. Once macaroni noodles are done, drain the noodles and place the stick of butter in the bottom of the baking dish
  6. Pour noodles over butter and toss until butter has melted completely
  7. Fold the shredded cheese into the noodles, leaving about 1 cup worth of cheese to the side (we'll use this later)
  8. Pour whisked eggs evenly over the cheese/noodle mix
  9. Use remaining cheese to evenly cover the top of the mixture

Bring The Heat

Mac

Place baking dish with mixture in oven. Cook until cheese on top is slightly brown/crispy

Note: Place Mac in oven BEFORE starting the burgers

Burgers

Place each patty directly on grilling surface. Grill until edges of burgers begin to curl slightly, flipping each patty intermittently.

Note: Turkey is a much leaner meat than beef. Overcooking will cause the burgers to be dry!
